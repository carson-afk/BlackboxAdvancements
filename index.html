LOCAL VS CLOUD

Why frontier AI lives in GPU farms — and why your simple tasks shouldn't.

A single Claude Opus 4.7 inference pass touches hundreds of GB of weights spread across dozens of H100s. That's why frontier AI is cloud-first. But 70% of your automation work doesn't need that kind of firepower.

CLOUD

GPU farms

Frontier models (Opus, GPT-5, Gemini) run on clusters of H100s and B200s. You rent capacity per-token. Smart for hard reasoning, tool use, and anything customer-facing.

Hardware8× H100 / B200 nodes
Inference cost$$$ per M tokens
Latency150–400ms first token
Best forReasoning, agents, writing
⇆
Smart router
LOCAL

Your hardware

Open-weight models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen) run on a single machine in your office. Zero per-token cost. Perfect for classification, extraction, summarization, routing.

Hardware1× RTX 4090 or Mac Studio
Inference cost$0 per token · electricity only
Latency30–80ms first token
Best forRouting, tagging, extraction

HYBRID COST SAVINGS

Route the easy tasks to free local models. Save the expensive ones for the brain.

80% of your AI workload is boring: classifying emails, extracting data, routing tickets, scoring leads. There's no reason to pay Opus pricing for that.

We deploy a tiny local model on your hardware for the easy 80%, and only escalate to Claude Opus 4.7 when the task actually needs a frontier brain. For a large operation, that's five figures a month in saved inference spend — and faster response times to boot.
