Systems

The architecture behind the outcome.

Blackbox Advancements is not a loose collection of automations. It is a connected business system with acquisition, CRM, portal, support, and execution layers working together.

  • Client-side visibility
  • Agent-side control
  • OpenClaw-powered local execution
Layer 01

Demand intake

Google Ads, Meta Ads, cold outreach, landing pages, websites, and AI-assisted social publishing feed a clean lead stream into the core system.

Layer 02

Qualification and booking

AI assistants, sequences, smart routing, and meeting scheduling convert raw interest into actionable pipeline movement.

Layer 03

CRM synchronization

Leads, notes, stage updates, reminders, and follow-up logic move automatically so the team is never guessing what happened last.

Layer 04

Client portal infrastructure

Customers can see arrival time, delivery timing, status updates, and custom account information through secure, branded portal experiences.

Layer 05

Agent-side portal tooling

Internal dashboards give agents and staff the right next actions, visibility, and controls to operate at speed without blind spots.

Layer 06

OpenClaw local node

An always-on business machine gives the company a stable local execution point so OpenClaw agents can run consistently inside the operation.

Client-side portal examples
  • Arrival or dispatch time visibility
  • Delivery or completion tracking
  • Project stage updates
  • Account-specific notifications
  • Secure login and status access
Agent-side portal examples
  • Live lead queue and response status
  • Call and outreach control panels
  • CRM action prompts
  • Scheduling and follow-up logic
  • Escalation, support, and handoff workflows
Why this matters: most automation fails because the sales layer, support layer, and operations layer are built separately. We install them as one system so the business actually feels faster from every angle.
Deployment standard

Installed locally, maintained continuously, designed to scale.

We configure OpenClaw inside the client business, connect the surrounding stack, secure access, and keep supporting the system after launch so it stays useful instead of decaying.
