FAQ · Common questions, direct answers

Questions, answered
without the sales pitch.

Homeowner questions we hear most often — about sizing, warranty, financing, timing, and what "owner-led" actually means. If yours isn't here, Dan will answer it on a phone call.

How fast can you get to me for a repair?
Same-day for existing customers in season, and usually next-day for new callers during shoulder seasons. For no-heat or no-cool emergencies, the line at (509) 525-1339 rings 24/7. Response time is one of the three things we measure ourselves on.
Do you give free estimates?
Yes — complimentary in-home assessments for installs, retrofits, and replacements. We walk the house, perform a proper Manual-J–style load look, and send a written proposal within a few business days. There's no obligation, and we don't hold estimates hostage behind a deposit.
What equipment do you install?
We lead with Mitsubishi and Daikin for ductless mini-splits, and Trane / American Standard / Goodman for traditional split systems depending on the homeowner's budget and the application. Rheem for water heaters. Gas fireplaces: primarily Kozy Heat, Heat & Glo, and Napoleon. We're brand-agnostic where it matters — right-sized and well-commissioned beats a big nameplate every time.
Is financing available?
Yes — we're an authorized Wells Fargo Home Projects® dealer. Approvals in minutes, $0 down on qualifying projects, fixed monthly payments, and no prepayment penalties. See the Financing page for an example payment calculator.
What warranties come with my install?
Standard 10-year manufacturer warranty on the equipment (most major brands), plus a 2-year workmanship guarantee from us on the install itself. Enroll in one of our annual maintenance plans and the workmanship coverage extends for the life of the plan.
How long does a full HVAC install take?
A straightforward furnace & AC changeout is usually one day. A heat-pump conversion with new line-sets and a condenser pad typically runs 1–2 days. A full new-duct install or multi-zone ductless project runs 2–4 days depending on home layout. We commission and walk the homeowner through the thermostat before we leave — that part is not rushed.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes — licensed and insured in Washington State. We're happy to furnish a current certificate of insurance on request for any project.
Do you work on commercial systems?
Yes — light commercial: rooftop package units, small office splits, restaurants, and tasting rooms. We don't chase large VAV/chilled-water buildings. If the project is a fit, we'll tell you; if it isn't, we'll tell you that too.
What rebates & incentives apply in the Valley?
We itemize every applicable rebate — manufacturer, Pacific Power / Columbia REA / PUD, and Energy Trust of Oregon (for Milton-Freewater customers) — directly in your proposal. High-efficiency heat pumps and heat-pump water heaters carry the largest rebates right now.
What does "owner-led" actually mean here?
It means Dan — the owner — performs the walk-through on every project, reviews the proposal before it goes out, and personally commissions every install before we invoice. You aren't handed off to a salesperson or a rotating crew. If something isn't right, you call Dan.
Do you service systems other companies installed?
Yes. Homeowners frequently come to us after a previous contractor has cycled through three techs who couldn't find the issue. Bring us the system — we'll diagnose it, document everything we find, and quote only what's needed.
What areas do you serve?
The entire Walla Walla Valley — Walla Walla, College Place, Milton-Freewater, Touchet, Lowden, Dayton, Waitsburg, Prescott, Burbank, and select projects in Pendleton. See our Service Areas page for details.
